The "Disagree and Commit" Rule

Intel and Amazon use this principle: You're required to voice disagreement during the debate. But once a decision is made, everyone commits 100%‚Äîeven those who disagreed.

This works because people were heard. They fought for their position. They lost fairly. Now they can commit.

The Mining for Conflict Technique

Great facilitators don't let conflict stay hidden. They actively pull it out: "I sense some people disagree. What are we not saying?" or "Let's hear the strongest argument against this."

Coaching vs Managing

Partner, don't direct

Coaching vs Managing

Managing tells people what to do. Coaching helps them figure it out. One creates compliance. The other creates ownership.

The Fundamental Shift
üëî Managing = "Here's what you should do"
You're the expert. You diagnose, prescribe, direct. Fast in the short term. Dependency in the long term. People wait for you to tell them what to do.
üå± Coaching = "What do you think?"
You're a partner. You ask, listen, reflect. Slower upfront. Ownership and growth long term. People develop their own thinking muscle.
When to Coach vs Manage
‚úÖ Coach When:
‚Ä¢ They have the capability (just need clarity or confidence)
‚Ä¢ You want to develop their skills
‚Ä¢ There's time to explore solutions
‚Ä¢ Building ownership matters more than speed
‚Ä¢ The relationship is strong enough for honest reflection
üö® Manage When:
‚Ä¢ Crisis mode (fire is burning, need action now)
‚Ä¢ They lack the knowledge or skill (training needed first)
‚Ä¢ Compliance is required (legal, safety, non-negotiable)
‚Ä¢ You've already tried coaching and it's not landing
Why Most Leaders Over-Manage
‚è±Ô∏è It feels faster
Telling is quick. But long term, you become the bottleneck. Every decision flows through you.
üí™ It feels powerful
Being the solver is addictive. You're needed. But it doesn't scale‚Äîand it disempowers your team.
üò∞ Coaching feels risky
What if they don't come up with the "right" answer? (News flash: there's often more than one right answer. And letting them find it builds capability.)
The Coaching Mindset
üß† They have the answer
Your job isn't to be the smartest person in the room. It's to unlock the thinking already in the room.
üéØ Curiosity > Certainty
Stay genuinely curious. "What do you think?" is more powerful than "Here's what I think."
ü§ù Partnership > Hierarchy
You're not above them. You're beside them, helping them think through it.

  • Notice: How does it feel to hold back your answer? What shifts?

The GROW Model

A simple coaching framework

The GROW Model

GROW is the most widely used coaching framework. Goal ‚Üí Reality ‚Üí Options ‚Üí Way Forward. Simple, effective, powerful.

G = Goal (What do you want?)
üéØ Clarify the Outcome
Start by getting clear on what they want. Not what you want‚Äîwhat they want. "What's the goal here?" / "What does success look like?"
üíé Make it Specific
Vague goal = vague action. Push for clarity. "By when?" / "What will you see/feel/hear when you've achieved it?"
‚ùì Sample Questions:
‚Ä¢ What do you want to achieve?
‚Ä¢ What would make this conversation successful?
‚Ä¢ If we could only focus on one thing, what would it be?
R = Reality (Where are you now?)
üìç Assess the Current State
Help them see their situation clearly. What's working? What's not? What have they tried? No judgment‚Äîjust exploration.
üîç Dig Deeper
Surface level = surface solutions. Go deeper. "What's really going on?" / "What's underneath that?"
‚ùì Sample Questions:
‚Ä¢ What's happening now?
‚Ä¢ What have you tried so far?
‚Ä¢ What's working? What's not?
‚Ä¢ What's stopping you?
O = Options (What could you do?)
üåà Brainstorm Possibilities
Generate options. Don't evaluate yet‚Äîjust create. The goal is quantity, not quality. "What else?" is your best friend here.
üö´ Resist Solving
This is where you'll be tempted to jump in with your brilliant idea. Don't. Let them generate options first. If they're stuck, ask: "What would you do if you had unlimited resources?" or "What would someone you admire do?"
‚ùì Sample Questions:
‚Ä¢ What are your options?
‚Ä¢ What else could you try?
‚Ä¢ If time/money weren't a constraint, what would you do?
‚Ä¢ What would [role model] do in this situation?
W = Way Forward (What will you do?)
‚úÖ Get Commitment
Move from thinking to action. "So what will you actually do?" Make it concrete. Specific actions, specific timelines.
üõ°Ô∏è Anticipate Obstacles
"What might get in the way?" Identify blockers now. Build contingency plans. This increases follow-through.
‚ùì Sample Questions:
‚Ä¢ What's your next step?
‚Ä¢ When will you do it?
‚Ä¢ What support do you need?
‚Ä¢ How will you know you're making progress?
‚Ä¢ On a scale of 1-10, how committed are you? (If below 8, explore what's holding them back)

Powerful Questions

Ask more, tell less

Start

Powerful Questions

The quality of your questions determines the quality of the thinking. Great coaches ask questions that unlock insight, not just information.

What Makes a Question "Powerful"?
üîì It Unlocks Thinking
Powerful questions open doors. They make people pause, reflect, go deeper. "What's the real challenge here for you?"
üéØ It's Open, Not Closed
Closed question = yes/no. "Did you finish the report?" Open question = exploration. "What progress have you made on the report?"
üß† It Provokes Insight
Weak question: "What happened?" (information). Powerful question: "What surprised you?" (reflection, learning).
The Coaching Question Arsenal
üåü To Clarify Goals
‚Ä¢ "What does success look like?"
‚Ä¢ "What's most important to you here?"
‚Ä¢ "If you could wave a magic wand, what would change?"
üîç To Explore Reality
‚Ä¢ "What's really going on?"
‚Ä¢ "What have you tried so far?"
‚Ä¢ "What's stopping you?"
‚Ä¢ "What's the hardest part?"
üí° To Generate Options
‚Ä¢ "What are your options?"
‚Ä¢ "What else could you try?"
‚Ä¢ "If you had no constraints, what would you do?"
‚Ä¢ "What would [someone they admire] do?"
üöÄ To Drive Action
‚Ä¢ "What's your next step?"
‚Ä¢ "When will you do that?"
‚Ä¢ "What support do you need?"
‚Ä¢ "On a scale of 1-10, how committed are you?" (If <8: "What would make it a 10?")
ü™û To Deepen Reflection
‚Ä¢ "What did you learn?"
‚Ä¢ "What surprised you?"
‚Ä¢ "If you could do it again, what would you change?"
‚Ä¢ "What does this tell you about yourself?"
The Secret Weapon: "What Else?"
üîÑ Keep Asking
Most people's first answer is surface level. The real insight comes on round 2 or 3. After they answer, pause, then: "What else?" Do it 2-3 times. Watch the depth increase.
Questions to Avoid
üö´ "Why?" (Often Feels Accusatory)
"Why did you do that?" ‚Üí Defensive. Try: "What led you to that decision?" Same info, softer tone.
üö´ Leading Questions
"Don't you think you should...?" You're not coaching‚Äîyou're telling with a question mark. Be direct if you have a point of view.
üö´ Multiple Questions at Once
"What happened? And what did they say? And what did you do?" Overwhelming. One question at a time. Then pause. Let them think.
The Pause: Your Most Powerful Tool
‚è∏Ô∏è After You Ask, Stop Talking
Silence feels uncomfortable. Resist the urge to fill it. The pause is where they do the thinking. 3-5 seconds of silence = gold. Let them process.

Giving Effective Feedback

Good feedback is specific, timely, and kind. It focuses on behavior, not character. It offers a path forward.

2Ô∏è‚É£ Behavior

3Ô∏è‚É£ Impact

Receiving Feedback

Receiving feedback well is a skill. The first instinct is to defend. The growth move is to listen, understand, and decide.

The Feedback Reception Protocol
üß† Listen without interrupting
Let them finish. Your job isn't to defend‚Äîit's to understand what they observed and how it landed for them.
ü§î Ask clarifying questions
"Can you give me an example?" or "What would that look like differently?" gets specifics.
‚úèÔ∏è Take notes
Calms your nervous system. Shows you're taking it seriously. Helps you remember later.
‚è∏Ô∏è Pause before responding
"Let me think about this" or "I'll get back to you" is fine. Impulse responses often regret.
What to Do After
üîç Assess it as data
Is there truth in it? Not all feedback is valuable, but consider it objectively.
üéØ Decide what to do
Act on it, consider it, or respectfully disagree. The choice is yours.
üôè Thank them
Even if you disagree, someone took time to give you perspective. Appreciation keeps feedback flowing.
üé¨ Practice: Common Reactions
Your manager: "Your reports are good but sometimes hard to follow. Add a summary at the top."
‚ùå Defensive: "I always put important info first. My last report was fine."
‚úÖ Growth: "That's helpful. Can you show me an example? I want to make them easier for you to use."

Feedback Culture

Psychological safety‚Äîthe belief that you can speak up without punishment‚Äîis where great feedback happens. You can start building it.

What Kills Psychological Safety
üî¥ Punishment for speaking up
If feedback is met with defensiveness or retaliation, people stop offering it.
üî¥ Perfectionism
If mistakes are treated as failures, people hide problems rather than surface them.
üî¥ Status plays
When high-status people dismiss low-status people's input, feedback disappears.
What Builds Psychological Safety
üü¢ Acknowledge your own mistakes
"I got that wrong. Here's what I learned." Signals it's safe to be human.
üü¢ Ask for feedback first
Vulnerability invites vulnerability. "What could I do better?" opens the door.
üü¢ Respond well to feedback
When someone gives feedback, thank them and show you're acting on it. That teaches safety.
üü¢ Treat people as learners
"You're growing in this" vs "You failed." One shuts down feedback, one opens it.

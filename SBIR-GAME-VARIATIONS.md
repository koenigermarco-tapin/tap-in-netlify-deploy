<a name="sbir-taboo"></a>
## üéÆ GAME 1: SBIR TABOO (Original)

**Difficulty:** ‚≠ê Beginner  
**Players:** 2-6  
**Duration:** 30-45 minutes  
**Belt Level:** White Belt+  
**Primary Focus:** SBIR fundamentals

### Overview:
Players take turns delivering SBIR feedback on scenario cards while avoiding forbidden words. Group votes on best delivery each round.

### Setup:
- All scenario cards shuffled by color
- Forbidden Word Cards (Tier 1 only for beginners)
- SBIR Reference Cards visible
- Score sheets for tracking

### Round Structure:
1. **Draw (15s):** Player selects color and draws card
2. **Prep (60s):** Silent planning time
3. **Deliver (90s):** Present complete SBIR to group
4. **Check (30s):** Group identifies any forbidden word violations
5. **Vote (30s):** Best SBIR of round gets bonus points
6. **Rotate:** Next player

### Winning:
- Highest total score
- Points from card difficulty + vote bonuses - abandoned cards

### Best For:
- First-time players
- Learning basic SBIR structure
- Building comfort with feedback delivery
- Foundation for all other variations

<a name="speed-sparring"></a>
## ‚ö° GAME 2: SPEED SPARRING

**Difficulty:** ‚≠ê‚≠ê Intermediate  
**Players:** 2-8  
**Duration:** 15-20 minutes  
**Belt Level:** Blue Belt+  
**Primary Focus:** Thinking quickly under pressure

### Overview:
Rapid-fire SBIR delivery with significantly reduced time limits. Tests ability to structure feedback quickly.

### Setup:
- Use only Green and Yellow cards
- Tier 1-2 Forbidden Words
- Timer clearly visible
- Fast-paced music optional (creates energy)

### Modified Timing:
- **Draw:** 10 seconds
- **Prep:** 20 seconds (vs. 60s in original)
- **Deliver:** 45 seconds (vs. 90s)
- **Check:** 15 seconds
- **Vote:** 15 seconds
- **Total per round:** ~2 minutes

### Special Rules:
- **No abandonment allowed** - must attempt every card drawn
- **Incomplete SBIR = 0 points** (must have all 4 elements)
- **Tie votes = no bonus** (encourages excellence, not adequacy)
- **10 rounds minimum** (volume over perfection)

### Scoring Variation:
- Green: 1 pt
- Yellow: 2 pts
- Vote bonus: +1 pt (not +0.5) to reward clear winners
- No penalties since abandonment isn't allowed

### Winning:
- Most points after 10 rounds
- Tiebreaker: Most Yellow cards delivered

### Advanced Variation: "Lightning Round"
- All prep time eliminated
- Read card, deliver immediately
- 30 seconds total per person

### Best For:
- Players who overthink/freeze
- Building spontaneity
- Simulating real-world pressure (no time to prepare)
- Energizing a tired group

### Facilitator Tips:
- Keep energy HIGH - this is meant to feel frenetic
- Don't stop for discussion during rounds
- Save all teaching for debrief
- Celebrate speed AND quality

<a name="partner-buyin"></a>
## ü§ù GAME 3: PARTNER BUY-IN CHALLENGE

**Difficulty:** ‚≠ê‚≠ê Intermediate  
**Players:** 4-8 (must be even number)  
**Duration:** 45-60 minutes  
**Belt Level:** Blue Belt+  
**Primary Focus:** Productive conflict, achieving buy-in

### Overview:
Pairs work together. One delivers SBIR, partner role-plays the receiver and must be convinced to buy in to the request.

### Setup:
- Divide into pairs
- Each pair draws one card per round
- Mix of Yellow, Orange, Red cards
- Tier 2-3 Forbidden Words

### Round Structure (15 minutes per round):

#### Phase 1: Preparation (5 min)
- Pair draws scenario card
- **Decide roles:**
  - **Giver:** Will deliver SBIR
  - **Receiver:** Will role-play the person receiving feedback
- Giver prepares SBIR
- Receiver prepares defensive responses (realistic, not absurd)

#### Phase 2: Delivery (3 min)
- Giver delivers SBIR
- Receiver responds authentically:
  - Can ask clarifying questions
  - Can express defensiveness
  - Can push back on the request
- Giver must achieve buy-in (receiver says "Yes, I'll do that")

#### Phase 3: Scoring (2 min)
- **Other pairs vote:**
  - Did the SBIR include all 4 elements? (2 pts)
  - Did they avoid forbidden words? (2 pts)
  - Did receiver authentically buy in? (3 pts)
  - Bonus: Would YOU buy in if you received this feedback? (2 pts)
- Maximum 9 points per round

#### Phase 4: Swap (5 min)
- Roles reverse
- New card drawn
- Repeat

### Special Rules:
- **Receiver must be authentic** - not cooperative OR impossible
- **Buy-in must be genuine** - saying "okay fine" doesn't count
- **Giver can revise** - if first attempt fails, can try again
- **Time cap:** 3 minutes to achieve buy-in or score 0 for that element

### Winning:
- Pair with highest combined score across rounds
- Demonstrates both delivery AND receiving skills

### Advanced Variation: "Hostile Receiver"
- Receiver starts defensive and resistant
- Giver must de-escalate AND deliver SBIR
- Extra 5 points if buy-in achieved

### Best For:
- Practicing dialogue, not monologue
- Learning to handle defensiveness
- Understanding receiver's perspective
- Blue Belt focus: Engaging in productive conflict

### Facilitator Tips:
- Coach receivers: "Be realistic, not sabotaging"
- Look for: Does giver stay in SBIR when pushed back?
- Highlight moments when defensive receiver softened
- Debrief: "What made you buy in vs. resist?"

<a name="escalation-ladder"></a>
## ü™ú GAME 4: ESCALATION LADDER

**Difficulty:** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê Advanced  
**Players:** 3-6  
**Duration:** 60 minutes  
**Belt Level:** Purple Belt+  
**Primary Focus:** Lack of Commitment dysfunction

### Overview:
Players start with Green cards and must successfully deliver before advancing to harder colors. Tests consistency and commitment under increasing difficulty.

### Setup:
- Separate decks by color
- Tier 1-3 Forbidden Words
- Each player starts at Green level
- Track progress on whiteboard

### Game Structure:

#### Level 1: Green (Easy)
- Must deliver 2 Green cards successfully
- Standard SBIR rules
- Any abandoned card resets to 0/2

#### Level 2: Yellow (Moderate)
- Must deliver 2 Yellow cards successfully
- Add Tier 2 restrictions (no vague language)
- Any abandoned card drops back to Level 1

#### Level 3: Orange (Advanced)
- Must deliver 1 Orange card successfully
- Add Tier 3 restrictions (no power violations)
- Abandoned card drops back to Level 2

#### Level 4: Red (Expert)
- Must deliver 1 Red card successfully
- Add Tier 4 restrictions (master level)
- Abandoned card drops back to Level 3

### Special Rules:
- **No voting bonuses** - focus is on personal progression, not competition
- **Other players can help** - between rounds, can coach
- **Abandoned = drop one level** (creates real stakes)
- **Can't skip levels** - must go Green ‚Üí Yellow ‚Üí Orange ‚Üí Red

### Winning Conditions:

**Individual Win:**
- First person to complete all 4 levels
- Get bonus challenge: Deliver perfect SBIR on any Red card with Tier 4 restrictions and receive unanimous "buy-in" votes

**Team Win:**
- Everyone reaches Red level
- Demonstrates collective commitment

### Scoring:
- Green completion: 5 pts
- Yellow completion: 10 pts
- Orange completion: 15 pts
- Red completion: 20 pts
- **Bonus:** Complete all 4 levels = +20 pts

### Advanced Variation: "The Gauntlet"
- Once at Red level, must deliver 3 Red cards in a row
- Any abandonment drops to Green
- First to complete wins eternal glory

### Best For:
- Purple Belt focus: Achieving buy-in through commitment
- Players who give up easily when challenged
- Building resilience through difficulty
- Creating progressive skill development

### Facilitator Tips:
- **Normalize dropping levels:** "This shows you're pushing your edge"
- **Celebrate micro-progress:** "You got 1/2 Yellows - farther than last time"
- **Group support:** Encourage coaching between rounds
- **Track visually:** Whiteboard shows everyone's level (creates accountability)

### Debrief Focus:
- "What made you commit to pushing through vs. giving up?"
- "How does this relate to Purple Belt: Lack of Commitment?"
- "Where in your life do you abandon challenges when they get hard?"

## üåç GAME 5: CULTURE CLASH

**Difficulty:** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê Advanced  
**Players:** 4-8  
**Duration:** 60-90 minutes  
**Belt Level:** Brown Belt+  
**Primary Focus:** Accountability across different contexts

### Overview:
Players must deliver same SBIR across different cultural/professional contexts, adjusting delivery while maintaining core message.

### Setup:
- Red and Orange cards only
- Context cards (see below)
- Partners or small groups
- Tier 3-4 Forbidden Words

### Context Cards (Create 12):

**Professional Contexts:**
1. üè¢ Startup (Casual, fast-paced)
2. üè¶ Corporate Banking (Formal, hierarchical)
3. üé® Creative Agency (Relationship-focused, emotional)
4. ‚öïÔ∏è Healthcare (Direct, patient safety stakes)

**Cultural Contexts:**
5. üá©üá™ German (Direct, process-oriented)
6. üáØüáµ Japanese (Indirect, face-saving)
7. üá∫üá∏ American (Individualistic, solution-focused)
8. üáßüá∑ Brazilian (Warm, relationship-first)

**Generational Contexts:**
9. üë¥ Boomer (Formal, loyalty-based)
10. üë® Gen X (Pragmatic, skeptical)
11. üë© Millennial (Purpose-driven, feedback-hungry)
12. üßë Gen Z (Authentic, boundaries-conscious)

### Round Structure (20 min per round):

#### Phase 1: Scenario & Context (2 min)
- Player draws scenario card (Orange or Red)
- Draws 3 different context cards
- Reads aloud to group

#### Phase 2: Preparation (5 min)
- Plans how SBIR would be delivered in each context
- Can consult with partner
- Notes key differences in approach

#### Phase 3: Triple Delivery (9 min - 3 min each)
- Delivers same SBIR in all 3 contexts
- Group identifies what changed and what stayed the same
- Must maintain all 4 SBIR elements in each

#### Phase 4: Evaluation (4 min)
- Group votes on whether delivery was appropriate for each context
- **Scoring:**
  - Context 1 appropriate: 3 pts
  - Context 2 appropriate: 3 pts
  - Context 3 appropriate: 3 pts
  - Maintained core SBIR structure in all: 5 pts
  - No forbidden words in any: 4 pts
- Maximum: 18 pts per round

### What Should Change Across Contexts:

**Can/Should Adapt:**
- ‚úì Formality of language
- ‚úì Directness vs. indirectness
- ‚úì Relationship-building before feedback
- ‚úì Tone and pacing
- ‚úì Frame (individual vs. team impact)

**Must Stay Consistent:**
- ‚úó The core behavior being addressed
- ‚úó The specific situation (facts don't change)
- ‚úó The measurable impact
- ‚úó The actionable request
- ‚úó All 4 SBIR elements present

### Example:

**Scenario:** Teammate missed critical deadline

**üè¢ Startup Context:**
"Hey, when you missed the Friday API deadline, it blocked the whole dev team for 3 days and we lost the investor demo slot. Can we set up checkpoint sync-ups on Tuesday/Thursday so we catch delays early?"

**üè¶ Corporate Banking Context:**
"I'd like to discuss the December 3rd deliverable. The submission was received on December 10th, which created a 7-day delay in the compliance review process and put us at risk of regulatory penalties. Going forward, I need you to escalate potential delays 48 hours in advance so we can adjust timelines appropriately."

**üáØüáµ Japanese Context:**
"Thank you for your hard work on the project. I wanted to share some observations. Regarding the Friday deadline, the delivery timing created some challenges for the team's workflow. Perhaps we could consider implementing a checkpoint system to support our collaboration? I would value your thoughts on this."

### Winning:
- Highest total score across rounds
- Demonstrates cultural intelligence + accountability skills

### Advanced Variation: "Context Surprise"
- Player prepares for one context
- Facilitator calls different context mid-delivery
- Must pivot on the spot

### Best For:
- Brown Belt focus: Accountability in diverse settings
- Global teams
- Leaders managing across generations/cultures
- Understanding how to maintain standards while respecting differences

### Facilitator Tips:
- **Don't stereotype:** Contexts are starting points, not rigid rules
- **Highlight principles:** "What stayed true across all contexts?"
- **Discuss over-adaptation:** "Did you lose the accountability in trying to be polite?"
- **Real examples:** Share stories of cross-cultural feedback failures/successes

### Debrief Focus:
- "Which context was hardest for you? Why?"
- "Where do you risk being too indirect? Too harsh?"
- "How do you maintain accountability without violating cultural norms?"
- "What's non-negotiable vs. adaptable in feedback?"

<a name="reverse-feedback"></a>
## üîÑ GAME 6: REVERSE FEEDBACK

**Difficulty:** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê Advanced  
**Players:** 4-8  
**Duration:** 45 minutes  
**Belt Level:** Brown Belt+  
**Primary Focus:** Receiving feedback gracefully

### Overview:
Players deliver intentionally mediocre SBIR. Others practice receiving it without defensiveness and improving the feedback together.

### Setup:
- Yellow and Orange cards
- Tier 2-3 Forbidden Words
- Pairs or small groups
- Focus on receiving, not delivering

### Round Structure (15 min per round):

#### Phase 1: Imperfect Delivery (3 min)
- Player A draws card
- Delivers SBIR but makes it **70% good**:
  - Includes all 4 elements BUT...
  - Situation is slightly vague
  - OR Behavior has some interpretation
  - OR Impact is unclear
  - OR Request is not specific enough
- **Should NOT use forbidden words** (that's too easy to catch)

#### Phase 2: Graceful Receiving (5 min)
- Player B receives the feedback
- **Must:**
  - ‚úì Listen without interrupting
  - ‚úì Acknowledge the impact
  - ‚úì Ask clarifying questions (not defensive ones)
  - ‚úì Thank them for the feedback
- **Cannot:**
  - ‚úó Defend their behavior
  - ‚úó Explain their intent
  - ‚úó Say "but..." or "however..."
  - ‚úó Minimize the impact

**Scoring for Receiving:**
- Listened fully: 2 pts
- Acknowledged impact: 2 pts
- Asked clarifying (not defensive) questions: 3 pts
- Thanked giver: 1 pt
- Stayed non-defensive throughout: 3 pts
Maximum: 11 pts

#### Phase 3: Collaborative Improvement (5 min)
- Together, Player A & B revise the SBIR to make it stronger
- Group provides input
- Final version is delivered again

**Scoring for Collaboration:**
- Improved Situation specificity: 2 pts
- Improved Behavior observability: 2 pts
- Improved Impact clarity: 2 pts
- Improved Request actionability: 2 pts
Maximum: 8 pts

#### Phase 4: Swap (2 min)
- Roles reverse
- New card
- Repeat

### Special Rules:
- **Facilitator evaluates receiving skill** - not just group vote
- **Bonus points:** Receiver shows vulnerability ("You're right, I do that")
- **Penalty:** Any defensive statement = -2 pts

### Defensive vs. Clarifying Questions:

**‚ùå Defensive (Penalty):**
- "Don't you think that's a bit harsh?"
- "But you don't know what I was dealing with..."
- "Are you sure it was THAT bad?"
- "Others don't seem to have a problem with it..."

**‚úÖ Clarifying (Points):**
- "Can you give me a specific example?"
- "When exactly did this happen?"
- "Help me understand the impact - what specifically changed?"
- "What would success look like if I did what you're requesting?"

### Winning:
- Highest combined score (receiving + collaborating)
- Shows humility + partnership

### Advanced Variation: "Hot Seat"
- One person receives feedback from entire group
- Each person delivers SBIR on a real behavior
- Receiver practices non-defensiveness under fire
- **Warning:** Only for psychologically safe, advanced groups

### Best For:
- Leaders who need to model receiving feedback
- Teams with defensiveness issues
- Brown Belt: Practicing accountability by accepting feedback
- Building vulnerability-based trust

### Facilitator Tips:
- **Model receiving first:** Let group give you real feedback
- **Catch defensive language immediately:** "That's a 'but' - try again"
- **Normalize discomfort:** "Your ego is screaming right now. That's normal."
- **Celebrate great receives:** "That was masterful - you just thanked someone for telling you something hard"

### Debrief Focus:
- "What did you notice happening in your body when receiving feedback?"
- "What made you want to defend vs. receive?"
- "How does your team respond when YOU give them feedback?"
- "What would change if everyone received feedback like this?"

<a name="gauntlet"></a>
## üî• GAME 7: THE FEEDBACK GAUNTLET

**Difficulty:** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê Expert  
**Players:** 5-10  
**Duration:** 45-60 minutes  
**Belt Level:** Black Belt  
**Primary Focus:** Sustained excellence under pressure

### Overview:
One player faces rapid-fire scenarios from the entire group. Must deliver quality SBIR on 5+ cards in a row without abandoning any.

### Setup:
- Mix of all colors (heavy on Orange/Red)
- All 4 tiers of Forbidden Words active
- Group forms semicircle around "hot seat"
- Timer, score sheet, high energy

### Round Structure (Player rotates into hot seat):

#### The Gauntlet Sequence (12 minutes):
1. **Card 1** - Green (Warmup)
   - 60 seconds total: 20s prep + 40s delivery
   
2. **Card 2** - Yellow
   - 60 seconds total: 15s prep + 45s delivery
   
3. **Card 3** - Orange
   - 60 seconds total: 10s prep + 50s delivery
   
4. **Card 4** - Red
   - 60 seconds total: 5s prep + 55s delivery
   
5. **Card 5** - Red (Same scenario as Card 4, different approach)
   - 60 seconds total: NO PREP + 60s delivery

**Continuous Challenge:**
- No breaks between cards
- Group actively watches for forbidden words
- Facilitator deals next card immediately after each delivery
- High-pressure, rapid pace

### Scoring:

**Completion Scoring:**
- Completes all 5 cards: 25 pts
- Each SBIR with all 4 elements: +3 pts (max 15)
- Zero forbidden words across all 5: +10 pts
- Group unanimously impressed (vote): +5 pts
- **Maximum possible:** 55 pts

**Failure Conditions:**
- Uses forbidden word: Game over, score halved
- Abandons any card: Game over, score = 0
- Incomplete SBIR (missing element): -5 pts per card
- Runs over time limit: -2 pts per card

### Special Rules:
- **Can't repeat same SBIR approach** - each delivery must show variety
- **Card 5 twist:** Same scenario, must use completely different framing
- **Group can heckle** (if player agrees beforehand):
  - "That's vague!"
  - "I don't buy in!"
  - "What does that even mean?"
  - Tests ability to stay composed under pressure

### Winning:
- Highest score after all players complete gauntlet
- Tiebreaker: Fastest total time

### Advanced Variation: "The Lightning Gauntlet"
- 10 cards instead of 5
- 30 seconds total per card
- Only Green/Yellow cards (speed over difficulty)
- First to complete without abandoned cards wins

### Ultra-Hard Variation: "The Master's Gauntlet"
- 7 cards: 1 Green, 1 Yellow, 2 Orange, 3 Red
- All Tier 4 restrictions
- Group role-plays hostile receivers
- Must achieve buy-in on final 3 Red cards

### Best For:
- Black Belt certification
- Demonstrating mastery
- Preparing for sustained difficult conversations
- Building extreme focus and composure

### Facilitator Tips:
- **Energy is everything:** This should feel intense
- **Encourage the group:** Create supportive pressure
- **Catch micro-improvements:** "You got more specific on each card"
- **Don't let them off easy:** If they want Black Belt, earn it

### Debrief Focus:
- "How did you manage the mental load of rapid deliveries?"
- "What changed from Card 1 to Card 5 in your delivery?"
- "Where in leadership do you face this kind of sustained pressure?"
- "Are you ready to teach this to others?"

<a name="speed-dating"></a>
## üíï GAME 8: SBIR SPEED DATING

**Difficulty:** ‚≠ê‚≠ê Intermediate  
**Players:** 6-12 (must be even)  
**Duration:** 45-60 minutes  
**Belt Level:** Blue Belt+  
**Primary Focus:** Volume and variety

### Overview:
Players rotate partners every 3 minutes. Each pairing delivers SBIR to each other on random scenarios. Maximizes practice volume.

### Setup:
- Two rows of chairs facing each other
- All scenario cards mixed
- Tier 1-2 Forbidden Words
- Bell or timer for rotations

### Round Structure (3 minutes per pairing):

#### Minute 1:
- Both players draw a card
- 30 seconds to read and prep

#### Minute 2:
- Player A delivers SBIR (45 seconds)
- Player B receives and provides quick feedback (15 seconds)

#### Minute 3:
- Player B delivers SBIR (45 seconds)
- Player A receives and provides quick feedback (15 seconds)

**Bell rings ‚Üí One row shifts left ‚Üí New pairing**

### Scoring:
- **Self-assessment after each delivery:**
  - Complete SBIR (all 4 elements): 1 pt
  - No forbidden words: 1 pt
  - Partner "bought in" (thumbs up): 1 pt
  - Maximum: 3 pts per delivery

### Rotations:
- 10 rotations = 10 different partners
- 20 total SBIR deliveries (10 given, 10 received)
- Each person gives feedback to 10 different people

### Special Rules:
- **No abandonments** - must deliver every card
- **Keep moving** - even if delivery was rough, bell means rotate
- **Energy stays high** - short bursts of focused practice
- **Track your own score** - honor system

### Winning:
- Highest self-reported score
- Bonus: "Most Helpful Feedback" voted by group

### Variations:

**Theme Rounds:**
- Rounds 1-3: Only Green cards (build confidence)
- Rounds 4-7: Only Yellow cards (increase difficulty)
- Rounds 8-10: Wild card (any color)

**Challenge Rounds:**
- Round 5: Both players deliver on same scenario
- Round 7: Swap cards (deliver on partner's scenario)
- Round 10: Red cards only (end on hardest)

### Best For:
- Large groups (efficient practice for many people)
- Building comfort with strangers
- High-volume repetition
- Energy and momentum when groups feel stuck

### Facilitator Tips:
- **Keep energy up:** Play upbeat music between rounds
- **Call time strictly:** Don't let conversations run over
- **Circulate during rounds:** Coach on the fly
- **Celebrate moments:** "I just heard a perfect Impact statement!"

### Debrief Focus:
- "How did your delivery change from partner 1 to partner 10?"
- "Which scenario type (color) was hardest? Why?"
- "What feedback did you receive that surprised you?"
- "Who gave you the most helpful feedback? What did they say?"

<a name="trust-tower"></a>
## üèóÔ∏è GAME 9: THE TRUST TOWER

**Difficulty:** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê Expert  
**Players:** 4-6  
**Duration:** 90 minutes  
**Belt Level:** Purple Belt+  
**Primary Focus:** Building trust through vulnerability

### Overview:
Players move from low-vulnerability (easy) scenarios to high-vulnerability (personal) scenarios. Each successful delivery "builds" trust, unlocking harder conversations.

### Setup:
- Scenario cards organized by vulnerability level
- Physical blocks/Jenga pieces (represents trust)
- Tier 1-3 Forbidden Words
- Intimate setting (this gets real)

### The Trust Tower Levels:

#### LEVEL 1: Foundation (Green Cards)
**Low Vulnerability** - Impersonal scenarios
- Office annoyances
- Neighbor conflicts
- Service issues
- Must successfully deliver 3 Green cards
- Each success = add 1 block to tower

#### LEVEL 2: Structure (Yellow Cards)
**Medium Vulnerability** - Work relationships
- Colleague conflicts
- Professional boundaries
- Team dynamics
- Must successfully deliver 2 Yellow cards
- Each success = add 2 blocks to tower

#### LEVEL 3: Framework (Orange Cards)
**High Vulnerability** - Personal relationships
- Family dynamics
- Close friendships
- Partnership issues
- Must successfully deliver 2 Orange cards
- Each success = add 3 blocks to tower

#### LEVEL 4: Peak (Red Cards + Real Scenarios)
**Extreme Vulnerability** - Truth-telling
- Real feedback to someone in the room
- OR extremely difficult Red card scenario
- Must deliver 1 successfully
- Success = add 5 blocks (tower should be 20+ blocks high)

### Round Structure:

**Individual Turn (15 minutes):**

1. **Choose level** (2 min)
   - Can attempt any unlocked level
   - Higher risk = higher reward
   - Discuss with group which level to try

2. **Prepare vulnerability** (5 min)
   - For Levels 1-3: Draw scenario card
   - For Level 4: Prepare real feedback OR hardest Red card
   - Group provides support: "You've got this"

3. **Deliver with authenticity** (5 min)
   - Deliver SBIR fully
   - Group provides feedback
   - **Vote:** Was this vulnerable and authentic?
   - Must be unanimous "yes" to add blocks

4. **Reflect** (3 min)
   - "What did that feel like?"
   - "What made you choose that level?"
   - "What are you avoiding?"

### Special Mechanics:

**Trust Collapse:**
- If forbidden word used: Remove 3 blocks from tower
- If delivery felt inauthentic: Remove 2 blocks
- If group senses avoidance: Remove 1 block
- If tower falls: Game ends, restart from Level 1

**Trust Acceleration:**
- If Level 4 real feedback delivered: Double all blocks
- If someone delivers feedback they've been avoiding for months: +10 blocks
- If entire group reaches Level 4: Victory condition met

### Scoring:
- Group score (collaborative, not competitive)
- Goal: Build tower to 25 blocks without collapse
- Represents: "Our collective trust is strong enough for hard conversations"

### Winning Condition:
**Victory:** Tower reaches 25 blocks with all players contributing

**Bonus Victory:** All players deliver Level 4 real feedback to each other

### Special Rules:
- **Can support but not save:** Group can encourage, but can't deliver for someone
- **Voluntary vulnerability:** No one forced to Level 4
- **Psychological safety paramount:** Facilitator can veto if getting unsafe
- **Debrief after each Level 4:** Process emotions before continuing

### Best For:
- Teams building vulnerability-based trust (White Belt focus)
- Long-term cohorts (not one-time workshops)
- Leadership teams ready for real feedback
- Purple Belt: Commitment requires trust

### Facilitator Tips:
- **Go slow:** This isn't a race
- **Watch for performance vulnerability:** Looking vulnerable ‚â† being vulnerable
- **Create safety first:** Don't pressure Level 4 if group isn't ready
- **Be ready for tears:** Real vulnerability brings real emotion
- **Have a plan for conflict:** Level 4 can surface real issues

### Debrief Focus:
- "What allowed you to be vulnerable here?"
- "What blocks you from this level of honesty in your daily life?"
- "How did it feel to receive Level 4 feedback?"
- "What changes if your team operates at this level of truth?"

### Warning:
**This game is intense.** Only facilitate if:
- Group has established safety
- You're prepared to handle real emotions
- Time is protected (can't rush this)
- Follow-up support is available

## üí° TIPS FOR CREATING CUSTOM VARIATIONS

### Start With:
1. **Learning objective:** What skill are you targeting?
2. **Constraint:** What makes it challenging?
3. **Structure:** How do rounds/rotations work?
4. **Scoring:** How do you measure success?
5. **Debrief:** What question will unlock the insight?

### Examples of Custom Variations Created by Facilitators:

**"The Appreciation Hack"** (White Belt)
- Deliver SBIR but start with genuine appreciation
- Tests: Can you combine appreciation + accountability?

**"The Emoji SBIR"** (Blue Belt - Remote Teams)
- Deliver SBIR via chat using only emojis first
- Then translate to verbal SBIR
- Tests: Clarity across communication mediums

**"The Boardroom"** (Brown/Black Belt)
- Deliver SBIR as if presenting to executive board
- Focus: Professional polish + accountability

**"The Time Machine"** (Purple Belt)
- Deliver feedback on something from 6 months ago
- Tests: Can you be specific about past events?

üìã The Teaching Framework

Four levels of developing others.

The Development Ladder

1

I Do, You Watch

Model the behavior. Let them observe mastery in action. Explain your thinking out loud.

2

I Do, You Help

Include them in the work. Let them contribute while you lead. Build confidence through participation.

3

You Do, I Help

Let them lead while you support. Provide guardrails, not answers. Allow productive struggle.

4

You Do, I Watch

Step back completely. Let them own the outcome. Intervene only for safety. Celebrate their success.

‚ùå Diminisher Behaviors

‚Ä¢ Always have the answer
‚Ä¢ Jump in to "fix" problems
‚Ä¢ Make all decisions
‚Ä¢ Create dependency
‚Ä¢ Hoard information

‚úÖ Multiplier Behaviors

‚Ä¢ Ask questions first
‚Ä¢ Let them struggle productively
‚Ä¢ Push decisions down
‚Ä¢ Create autonomy
‚Ä¢ Share knowledge freely

ü•ã The 70-20-10 Rule

Research shows people develop: 70% from experience, 20% from coaching and feedback, and only 10% from formal training.

The best way to teach is to give people challenges just beyond their current ability‚Äîwith support, not answers.

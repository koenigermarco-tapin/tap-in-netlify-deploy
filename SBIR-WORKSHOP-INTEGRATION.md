### How SBIR Games Support the 5 Dysfunctions Journey

The SBIR Feedback Sparring Games are designed to build the specific skills needed at each belt level, progressing through Lencioni's 5 Dysfunctions of a Team in reverse order (from foundation to peak):

```
ðŸ¥‹ WHITE BELT    â†’ Absence of Trust (Vulnerability-Based Trust)
ðŸ¥‹ BLUE BELT     â†’ Fear of Conflict (Productive Conflict)
ðŸ¥‹ PURPLE BELT   â†’ Lack of Commitment (Buy-In & Clarity)
ðŸ¥‹ BROWN BELT    â†’ Avoidance of Accountability (Peer Accountability)
ðŸ¥‹ BLACK BELT    â†’ Inattention to Results (Collective Results via Teaching)
```

### Integration Philosophy:

**Games are not standalone activities** - they are skill-building drills integrated into the comprehensive belt curriculum:

- âœ… **Reinforce assessment learnings** - Apply concepts from belt assessments
- âœ… **Provide safe practice** - Low-stakes environment for high-stakes skills
- âœ… **Build muscle memory** - Repetition creates automaticity
- âœ… **Create community** - Shared experience builds team cohesion
- âœ… **Make learning sticky** - Play creates memorable moments

### When to Use SBIR Games:

| Context | Frequency | Purpose |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| Belt Training Programs | Weekly | Skill progression |
| Team Retreats | 1-2 sessions | Intensive practice |
| Leadership Workshops | Half-day | Foundational learning |
| Executive Coaching | Monthly | Accountability maintenance |
| Onboarding | Once per new cohort | Culture building |
| Refresher Training | Quarterly | Skill maintenance |

## ðŸ¥‹ BELT SYSTEM INTEGRATION

### Progression Through Belts:

Each belt level builds on previous skills while introducing new complexity:

| Belt | Primary Game Focus | Forbidden Tiers | Typical Duration | Success Metric |
|------|-------------------|-----------------|------------------|----------------|
| White | SBIR fundamentals + vulnerability | Tier 1 only | 4-6 weeks | Can deliver complete SBIR on Green/Yellow cards |
| Blue | Handling conflict + resistance | Tiers 1-2 | 4-6 weeks | Can achieve buy-in on Yellow/Orange cards |
| Purple | Sustained commitment under pressure | Tiers 1-3 | 4-6 weeks | Can deliver Orange/Red without abandoning |
| Brown | Accountability across contexts | Tiers 1-4 | 6-8 weeks | Can adapt SBIR to different cultures/situations |
| Black | Teaching + mastery demonstration | All tiers | Ongoing | Can facilitate games + coach others |

### Skill Accumulation:

**White Belt Foundation:**
- Knows all 4 SBIR elements
- Avoids blame language
- Delivers on low-stakes scenarios

**Blue Belt (White + New Skills):**
- Everything from White Belt PLUS:
- Handles defensive responses
- Avoids vague language
- Engages in productive conflict

**Purple Belt (White + Blue + New Skills):**
- Everything from White + Blue PLUS:
- Maintains consistency under pressure
- Avoids power language violations
- Creates clarity and commitment

**Brown Belt (White + Blue + Purple + New Skills):**
- Everything from previous belts PLUS:
- Adapts to different contexts
- Masters all forbidden word tiers
- Holds others accountable

**Black Belt (All Previous + Teaching):**
- Everything from all belts PLUS:
- Teaches SBIR to others
- Facilitates games
- Coaches real-time improvement
- Models mastery consistently

<a name="white-belt"></a>
## ðŸ¥‹ WHITE BELT INTEGRATION

### Dysfunction Focus: Absence of Trust (Vulnerability-Based Trust)

**Core Principle:** Trust comes from vulnerability. SBIR requires admitting "this impacted me" (vulnerability) rather than "you're wrong" (judgment).

### Learning Objectives:

By the end of White Belt, participants can:
- âœ… Identify all 4 SBIR elements in feedback examples
- âœ… Deliver complete SBIR on Green/Yellow scenarios
- âœ… Avoid Tier 1 forbidden words (blame language)
- âœ… Describe why vulnerability builds trust
- âœ… Give feedback without character attacks

### Recommended Games:

**Primary Game: SBIR Taboo (Original)**
- Use only Green cards for first 2 sessions
- Add Yellow cards in sessions 3-4
- Tier 1 restrictions only
- Focus on completeness over polish

**Supporting Game: Trust Tower (Foundation Level)**
- Build trust through successful deliveries
- Start with Level 1-2 only (Green/Yellow)
- Don't pressure Level 3-4 yet
- Use to demonstrate vulnerability â†’ trust connection

### Session Sequence (6 sessions, 90 min each):

#### Session 1: SBIR Foundations
**Before Games (45 min):**
- Review Lencioni's Trust dysfunction
- Teach SBIR framework with examples
- Demonstrate one complete round
- Answer questions

**Game Play (30 min):**
- SBIR Taboo: 5 rounds, Green cards only
- Tier 1 forbidden words
- Focus: Complete all 4 elements

**Debrief (15 min):**
- "Which element was hardest? Why?"
- "How does SBIR compare to how you normally give feedback?"
- "Where do you default to blame language?"

---

#### Session 2: Behavior vs. Character
**Before Games (20 min):**
- Review common Tier 1 violations from Session 1
- Practice converting character judgments to behavior descriptions
- Examples: "lazy" â†’ "submitted report 3 days late"

**Game Play (50 min):**
- SBIR Taboo: 7 rounds, Green + Yellow cards
- Strict enforcement of Tier 1
- Peer coaching between rounds

**Debrief (20 min):**
- "What's the difference between describing behavior vs. judging character?"
- "Why does behavior focus build trust while judgment destroys it?"
- "Give an example from your life this week."

---

#### Session 3: Impact Statements
**Before Games (20 min):**
- Focus on the "I" in SBIR
- Practice connecting behavior to measurable impact
- Three types: Personal, Team, Business impact

**Game Play (50 min):**
- SBIR Taboo: 7 rounds, mix of colors
- Voting criteria: Best impact statement gets bonus
- Track: "vague" vs. "specific" impact statements

**Debrief (20 min):**
- "How does sharing impact create vulnerability?"
- "When you admitted something affected you, how did that feel?"
- "How does the group respond when you're vulnerable?"

---

#### Session 4: Requests That Work
**Before Games (20 min):**
- Focus on the "R" in SBIR
- Vague vs. specific requests
- Actionable vs. abstract requests
- Practice making measurable requests

**Game Play (50 min):**
- SBIR Taboo: 7 rounds
- Peer voting: "Would you know how to comply with that request?"
- Track actionability

**Debrief (20 min):**
- "What makes a request actionable?"
- "Why is specificity an act of care?"
- "Where do you make vague requests? Why?"

---

#### Session 5: Putting It All Together
**Before Games (15 min):**
- Quick review of all 4 elements
- Common mistakes review
- Pep talk: "You're ready"

**Game Play (60 min):**
- SBIR Taboo: 10 rounds (longer session)
- All Green/Yellow cards
- Track personal best scores

**Debrief (15 min):**
- "How has your feedback changed since Session 1?"
- "Where have you used SBIR in real life?"
- "What's still hard?"

---

#### Session 6: White Belt Assessment
**Assessment (60 min):**
- Each person delivers SBIR on 3 scenarios:
  - 1 Green (easy)
  - 1 Yellow (moderate)
  - 1 chosen by facilitator
- Must avoid all Tier 1 violations
- Scored by group + facilitator

**Passing Criteria:**
- 2/3 scenarios delivered successfully
- All 4 SBIR elements present in each
- Zero Tier 1 violations
- Group consensus: "Ready for Blue Belt"

**Debrief (30 min):**
- Individual feedback on strengths/growth areas
- Commitment: One real SBIR conversation this week
- Preview Blue Belt: Productive conflict

---

### White Belt Success Indicators:

**Participant can:**
- âœ… Explain why trust requires vulnerability
- âœ… Describe all 4 SBIR elements from memory
- âœ… Catch Tier 1 violations in others' feedback
- âœ… Deliver complete SBIR on personal scenarios

## ðŸ¥‹ BLUE BELT INTEGRATION

### Dysfunction Focus: Fear of Conflict (Productive Conflict)

**Core Principle:** Conflict is productive when it focuses on ideas/behaviors, not personalities. SBIR enables "disagree and commit" by separating person from problem.

### Learning Objectives:

By the end of Blue Belt, participants can:
- âœ… Deliver SBIR when facing resistance
- âœ… Achieve buy-in from defensive receivers
- âœ… Avoid vague language (Tier 2)

### Recommended Games:

**Primary Game: Partner Buy-In Challenge**
- Simulates resistance
- Practices persistence without power violations
- Builds "disagree and commit" skills

**Supporting Games:**
- Speed Sparring (builds quick thinking under pressure)
- SBIR Speed Dating (volume of practice with different personalities)
- SBIR Taboo with Tier 2 restrictions

### Session Sequence (6 sessions, 90 min each):

#### Session 1: Introduction to Productive Conflict
**Before Games (30 min):**
- Review Lencioni's Conflict dysfunction
- Teach: Artificial harmony vs. productive conflict
- Conflict continuum: Avoidance â†’ Attack â†’ Productive
- SBIR as conflict enabler

**Game Play (40 min):**
- SBIR Taboo: 5 rounds

**Debrief (20 min):**
- "When did you feel conflict during delivery?"
- "How did SBIR structure help vs. hurt?"
- "Where do you avoid conflict in your team?"

---

#### Session 2: Handling Resistance
**Before Games (20 min):**
- Teach defensive response patterns
- How to stay in SBIR when challenged
- Practice: Giver delivers, receiver pushes back

**Game Play (50 min):**
- Partner Buy-In Challenge: 4 rounds
- Receivers roleplay realistic resistance
- Givers must achieve buy-in

**Debrief (20 min):**
- "What made you defensive as a receiver?"
- "What helped you buy in?"
- "How did you stay calm as a giver when pushed back?"

---

#### Session 3: Specificity Under Pressure
**Before Games (20 min):**
- Focus on Tier 2: Vague language
- Practice converting vague to specific
- Examples: "sometimes" â†’ "3 times this week"

**Game Play (50 min):**
- Speed Sparring: 10 rounds
- Strict Tier 2 enforcement
- Catch "vague" language immediately

**Debrief (20 min):**
- "Why is specificity harder when under pressure?"
- "How does vagueness avoid conflict?"
- "What's the risk of vague feedback?"

---

#### Session 4: Mining for Conflict
**Before Games (25 min):**
- Teach Blue Belt concept: "Mining"
- How to draw out disagreement
- Practice: "What are you not saying?"

**Game Play (45 min):**
- Partner Buy-In Challenge: 5 rounds
- Added rule: Giver must "mine" for hidden concerns
- Receiver must voice real objection before buying in

**Debrief (20 min):**
- "What conflict was hiding beneath compliance?"
- "How did mining change the conversation?"
- "Where do people on your team 'nod and ignore'?"

---

#### Session 5: The 7-Step Conflict Process
**Before Games (25 min):**
- Teach Blue Belt 7-Step Process
- SBIR maps to steps 2-5
- Practice full 7-step flow

**Game Play (45 min):**
- Partner Buy-In Challenge: Extended rounds (7 min each)
- Must use full 7-step process
- Partner scores each step

**Debrief (20 min):**
- "Which step was hardest?"
- "How does structure help conflict feel safer?"
- "What happens when you skip a step?"

---

#### Session 6: Blue Belt Assessment
**Assessment (60 min):**
- Each person faces 3 scenarios with hostile receiver:
  - 1 Yellow (moderate resistance)
  - 1 Orange (high resistance)
  - 1 Red (extreme resistance)
- Must achieve buy-in on 2/3
- Tiers 1-2 restrictions

**Passing Criteria:**
- Achieves authentic buy-in on 2/3 scenarios
- Zero Tier 1-2 violations
- Uses 7-step process
- Stays composed when challenged
- Group consensus: "Ready for Purple Belt"

**Debrief (30 min):**
- Individual feedback
- Commitment: Engage in one avoided conflict this week
- Preview Purple Belt: Commitment through clarity

---

### Blue Belt Success Indicators:

- âœ… Deliver SBIR when receiver is defensive

**Debrief (20 min):**
- "Why is 'improve' not a commitment?"
- "How do deadlines create clarity?"
- "Where do you avoid setting deadlines? Why?"

---

#### Session 5: Cascading Commitments
**Before Games (25 min):**
- Teach: How one commitment enables others
- Team alignment through clear commitments
- Practice: Linking individual commitments to team goals

**Game Play (45 min):**

- Each commitment builds on previous
- Group tracks how trust + commitment compound

**Debrief (20 min):**
- "How did clarity build momentum?"
- "What happens when one person breaks commitment?"
- "How does your team track commitments?"

### Session Sequence (8 sessions, 90 min each):

#### Session 1: Peer Accountability Foundations
**Before Games (30 min):**
- Review Lencioni's Accountability dysfunction

- Difference between manager accountability and peer accountability

**Game Play (40 min):**

- "When is it hardest to hold a peer accountable?"
- "What's the risk of avoiding peer accountability?"
- "How does your team currently handle underperformance?"

#### Session 2: Calling Out vs. Calling In
**Before Games (25 min):**
- Teach Brown Belt concept: Calling in with care

**Game Play (45 min):**

- Same feedback, different settings

**Debrief (20 min):**
- "When did public feedback feel appropriate? Inappropriate?"
- "How do you preserve dignity while holding standards?"
- "Where have you seen public callouts damage teams?"

---

#### Session 3: Cross-Cultural Accountability
**Before Games (25 min):**
- Review directness continuum across cultures
- How to maintain accountability without violating norms

**Game Play (45 min):**
- Culture Clash: Full game
- Must deliver in 3+ different cultural contexts
- Core message stays, delivery adapts

**Debrief (20 min):**
- "What stayed consistent? What changed?"
- "Where might you be too direct? Too indirect?"
- "How do you balance respect and accountability?"

---

#### Session 4: Hierarchical Accountability
**Before Games (25 min):**
- Teach: Holding a boss accountable (upward SBIR)
- Power dynamics and psychological safety
- When/how to escalate vs. address directly

**Game Play (45 min):**
- Partner Buy-In: Boss/subordinate scenarios
- Role-play power imbalances
- Practice maintaining respect while holding accountable

**Debrief (20 min):**
- "What makes upward accountability scary?"
- "How did SBIR structure help?"
- "When would you escalate vs. address directly?"

---

#### Session 5: Receiving Accountability
**Before Games (25 min):**
- Review Brown Belt concept: Accountability as gift
- How to receive hard feedback without defensiveness
- Practice: "Thank you for telling me"

**Game Play (45 min):**
- Reverse Feedback: Full game
- Focus on receiving, not delivering
- Score based on non-defensiveness

**Debrief (20 min):**
- "What made you defensive? What helped you stay open?"
- "How do YOU respond when held accountable?"
- "What changes if you model receiving accountability well?"

---

#### Session 6: Master Level Precision
**Before Games (25 min):**
- Introduce Tier 4 restrictions

**Game Play (45 min):**
- SBIR Taboo: Red cards only
- All 4 tiers active
- Strict enforcement of Tier 4

**Debrief (20 min):**
- "What words did you rely on that you can't use?"
- "How does precision increase or decrease impact?"
- "Where do you hedge when you should be direct?"

---

#### Session 7: High-Stakes Scenarios
**Before Games (20 min):**
- Review high-stakes SBIR examples
- Legal considerations (discrimination, harassment, etc.)
- When to involve HR/legal vs. peer accountability

**Game Play (50 min):**
- Culture Clash or Partner Buy-In: Red cards only
- Business scenarios with real consequences
- Must demonstrate professional composure

**Debrief (20 min):**
- "How did stakes change your delivery?"
- "What helped you stay composed?"
- "Where might you avoid these conversations in real life?"

---

#### Session 8: Brown Belt Assessment
**Assessment (70 min):**
- Three scenarios:
  1. **Peer accountability** (Orange card, Tiers 1-4)
  2. **Cross-cultural** (Red card, must deliver in 2 contexts)
  3. **Receiving accountability** (Take feedback without defending)
- All four tiers active

**Passing Criteria:**
- Successfully delivers scenarios 1-2 with zero violations
- Receives scenario 3 gracefully (facilitator scores)
- Demonstrates contextual adaptation
- Group consensus: "Ready for Black Belt"

**Debrief (20 min):**
- Individual feedback
- Commitment: Hold one peer accountable this week
- Preview Black Belt: Teaching others

### Black Belt Sequence (Ongoing Mastery):

Black Belt is not a 6-8 week program like other belts. It's an ongoing practice of:
1. **Demonstrating mastery**
2. **Teaching others**
3. **Maintaining culture**

---

#### Black Belt Certification Process:

### Part 1: Mastery Demonstration (2 hours)

**The Feedback Gauntlet:**
- Must complete full gauntlet (5+ cards including Red)
- All 4 tiers active
- Zero abandonments
- Score 45+ points
- Unanimous group approval

**Criteria:**
- Perfect SBIR structure on all deliveries
- Zero forbidden words across all tiers
- Professional composure under pressure
- Handles curveballs gracefully
- Sets example for others

---

### Part 2: Teaching Demonstration (4 hours)

**Facilitate a Full SBIR Session:**
- Choose a game variation
- Lead White or Blue Belt group
- Handle questions and challenges
- Deliver effective debrief

**Criteria:**
- Clear explanations of concepts
- Effective demonstration
- Creates psychological safety
- Catches violations accurately
- Coaches improvement in real-time
- Extracts insights during debrief

---

### Part 3: Culture Building (Ongoing)

**Embed SBIR in Team Culture:**
- Regular game sessions (monthly minimum)
- SBIR used in team meetings
- New members trained on SBIR
- Peer accountability normalized
- Feedback is expected and appreciated

**Evidence:**
- Team members use SBIR vocabulary
- Conflict is addressed constructively
- Accountability is peer-to-peer
- Results improve due to feedback culture
- Other teams notice the difference

---

### Black Belt Ongoing Practices:

#### Monthly: Maintain Your Edge
- Participate in advanced games (Gauntlet, Trust Tower Level 4)
- Take on hardest scenarios
- Model receiving feedback
- Push your own growth edge

#### Quarterly: Teach Others
- Facilitate SBIR games for your team
- Coach individuals struggling with feedback
- Create custom scenarios relevant to your context
- Contribute to SBIR game library

#### Annually: Level Up the Organization
- Train new facilitators
- Integrate SBIR into company onboarding
- Share success stories and metrics
- Refine games based on your learnings

## ðŸ”§ TROUBLESHOOTING BY BELT LEVEL

### White Belt Common Issues:

**Issue: "I don't know what Impact to state"**
- **Cause:** Hasn't reflected on how behavior affected them
- **Solution:** Pre-game exercise: Write 3 behaviors that affected you this week and name the impact

**Issue: Keeps using "you always" despite knowing it's forbidden**
- **Cause:** Habit pattern, not intentional
- **Solution:** Immediate catch and correction. Make them redeliver immediately.

**Issue: SBIR feels robotic/scripted**
- **Cause:** Too focused on format, not enough on authenticity
- **Solution:** "It's supposed to feel awkward at first. Authenticity comes with repetition."

---

### Blue Belt Common Issues:

**Issue: Gives up when receiver pushes back**
- **Cause:** Conflict avoidance, fear of damaging relationship
- **Solution:** Reframe: "Avoiding conflict damages the relationship more than engaging in it."

**Issue: Becomes vague when pressured**
- **Cause:** Discomfort with being direct
- **Solution:** Practice specificity drills. "Tell me the exact date and time."

**Issue: Reverts to Tier 1 violations under stress**
- **Cause:** Pressure reveals default patterns
- **Solution:** Slow down. Take a breath. Re-start the sentence.

---

### Purple Belt Common Issues:

**Issue: Abandons cards when difficulty increases**
- **Cause:** Low tolerance for sustained challenge
- **Solution:** Escalation Ladder with support. "The point is to push through."

**Issue: Requests lack specificity**
- **Cause:** Doesn't think through actionability
- **Solution:** "Who does what by when?" forced framework

**Issue: Gets explicit "yes" but person doesn't follow through**
- **Cause:** Didn't create real buy-in, just compliance
- **Solution:** "What would get in the way of you doing this?" Pre-mortem the commitment.

---

### Brown Belt Common Issues:

**Issue: Too direct in high-context cultures, too indirect in low-context**
- **Cause:** Doesn't read cultural cues
- **Solution:** Culture Clash game + discussion of directness continuum

**Issue: Can't hold boss accountable (upward SBIR)**
- **Cause:** Fear of retaliation or damaging career
- **Solution:** Acknowledge legitimate risk. Practice "Request for conversation" approach.

**Issue: Defensive when receiving accountability**
- **Cause:** Easier to give than receive
- **Solution:** Reverse Feedback game. Model receiving well.

---

### Black Belt Common Issues:

**Issue: Can perform but can't teach**
- **Cause:** Unconscious competence - doesn't remember what it's like to not know
- **Solution:** Observe White Belt struggles. Remember your journey.

**Issue: Impatient with learners**
- **Cause:** High standards, forgets learning takes time
- **Solution:** "Your job is to create the conditions for learning, not to BE the learning."

**Issue: Team doesn't sustain SBIR culture without facilitator present**
- **Cause:** Hasn't embedded practices in systems
- **Solution:** Create rituals. "Start every 1-on-1 with 'Any SBIR for me?'"

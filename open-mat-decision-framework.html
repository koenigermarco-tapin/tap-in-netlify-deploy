Decision-Making Framework

Stop spinning ‚Äî get clarity on hard choices

When to Use This

Use this framework for big, unclear decisions:

  • Should I take this job offer?
  • Should I start this business?
  • Should I move cities?
  • Should I end this relationship?
  • Should I pursue this opportunity?

Don't use it for: Routine decisions, choices you've already made emotionally (you're just seeking validation), or things that don't matter.

What decision are you facing?
1. Regret Minimization (10-10-10)
How will you feel about this decision in 10 minutes?
How will you feel about this decision in 10 months?
How will you feel about this decision in 10 years?
This is where clarity lives. What does your 10-year-older self want you to do?
2. Second-Order Thinking
If you say YES, what happens next? And then what?
Don't just think one move ahead. Play the tape forward 3-5 moves.
If you say NO, what happens next? And then what?
3. Values Alignment
What are your top 3 values right now?
e.g., Freedom, Growth, Family, Impact, Security, Adventure...
Which option better aligns with those values?
4. Fear vs Intuition Test
Are you leaning toward this choice because it excites you, or because you're afraid of the alternative?
What would you do if you weren't afraid?

üí° Decision-Making Principles
  • Reversible vs Irreversible: If it's reversible, decide fast. If it's irreversible, take your time.
  • Opportunity cost: Saying yes to this means saying no to something else. What are you giving up?
  • The rule of hell yeah or no: If it's not a "hell yeah," it's a no. (Use for non-essential commitments.)
  • Advice from future you: Imagine you're 80 years old. What would you tell yourself to do?
  • Sleep on it: Big decisions deserve overnight processing. Your subconscious is smarter than you think.

üìä The Research

Michael Porter defined strategy as "choosing what NOT to do." His research showed that competitive advantage comes from being different, not being better at the same thing everyone else does.

Strategy is sacrifice. If you're not saying no to things, you don't have a strategy.

The Strategy Triad

Every real strategy answers three questions:

  • Where do we play? ‚Äî What markets, customers, geographies?
  • How do we win? ‚Äî What's our competitive advantage?
  • What capabilities do we need? ‚Äî What must we be great at?

If your strategy is "be the best at everything for everyone"‚Äîyou don't have a strategy. You have a wish list.

üí° The "What Would Have to Be True?" Test

For any strategic choice, ask: "What would have to be true for this to be the right answer?" This surfaces assumptions and risks you might be ignoring.

Maintaining Boundaries

Start

Maintaining Boundaries

People will test new boundaries. That's normal. How you respond determines whether they stick.

When People Push Back
The Guilt Trip
"You're being selfish" or "You never used to be like this." Response: "I know this is new. This is important to me."
The Exception Request
"Just this once?" Response: "I appreciate the ask, but my boundary is firm. I can help you with X instead."
The Escalation
Someone goes around you or complains to your manager. Response: Stay calm and repeat your boundary.
The Ghosting
They get quiet or distant. This is actually the boundary working‚Äîpeople who respected you stay.
üéØ Practice Response
Your colleague: "I really need your help tonight"
You've set a boundary of no work after 6pm. What do you say?
üí° Maintenance Tips:
  • Enforce your boundary EVERY time, especially the first time someone tests it
  • Don't over-explain or apologize for your boundaries
  • Remember: People who care about you will respect your boundaries
  • Track how many times you upheld your boundary this week

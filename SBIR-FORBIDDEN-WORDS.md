### When to Use Each Tier:

**TIER 1 (Blame Language):**
- Training sessions
- Green card scenarios
- New players learning SBIR
- Focus: Building basic habits

**TIER 2 (Vague Language):**
- Intermediate practice
- Yellow card scenarios
- Players comfortable with Tier 1
- Focus: Precision and specificity

**TIER 3 (Power Language):**
- Advanced scenarios
- Orange card scenarios
- Sensitive relationship dynamics
- Focus: Safety and agency

**TIER 4 (Master Level):**
- Expert-level play
- Red card scenarios
- High-stakes business situations
- Black Belt training
- Focus: Professional precision

### Progression Path:

```
Week 1-2:   Master Tier 1 only
Week 3-4:   Add Tier 2
Week 5-6:   Add Tier 3
Week 7+:    Add Tier 4 for advanced players
```

### Facilitator Tips:

1. **Start with one tier** - Don't overwhelm new players
2. **Post the forbidden words** - Make them visible during play
3. **Catch and correct** - Stop play immediately when forbidden words are used
4. **Explain the why** - Help players understand the principle, not just the rule
5. **Celebrate good catches** - Reward players who spot violations

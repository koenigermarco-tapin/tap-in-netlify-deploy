McKinsey research: 70% of organizational transformations fail to achieve their goals. Not because the strategy was wrong‚Äîbut because the change was managed wrong.

Most change fails not from lack of planning, but from lack of understanding how humans actually change.

The Real Reason Change Fails

People don't resist change. They resist being changed.

When change is done TO people instead of WITH them, resistance is guaranteed. The best strategy in the world can't overcome people who feel threatened, ignored, or dismissed.

üìã Kotter's 8 Steps

The proven framework for leading change.

8 Steps to Transforming Your Organization

1

Create Urgency

Help others see the need for change and the importance of acting immediately.

2

Build a Guiding Coalition

Assemble a group with enough power to lead the change effort.

3

Form a Strategic Vision

Create a vision to direct the change and strategies for achieving it.

4

Enlist a Volunteer Army

Communicate the vision so people understand and accept it.

5

Enable Action by Removing Barriers

Remove obstacles, change systems that undermine the vision.

6

Generate Short-Term Wins

Plan for and create visible performance improvements.

7

Sustain Acceleration

Use credibility from wins to change systems and policies that don't fit.

8

Institute Change

Anchor new approaches in the culture for lasting change.

üí° The Short-Term Wins Secret

Step 6 is where most leaders fail. You need visible wins within 6-18 months‚Äîor momentum dies. Plan for them. Engineer them if you have to. Without wins, cynics win.

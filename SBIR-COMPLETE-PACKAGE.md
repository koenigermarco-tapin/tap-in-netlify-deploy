## üì¶ FILE 3: SBIR REFERENCE CARD

### THE SBIR FRAMEWORK

**S**ituation ‚Ä¢ **B**ehavior ‚Ä¢ **I**mpact ‚Ä¢ **R**equest

**Purpose:** Deliver feedback that creates change, not defensiveness. Build trust through vulnerability, not attack.

**Core Principle:** Focus on observable behavior and its impact, not character judgments or assumptions about intent.

---

### S - SITUATION

**What:** Set the context with specific time/place

**‚úÖ Good Examples:**
- "In yesterday's 2pm standup meeting..."
- "Last Tuesday when we were discussing the Q3 budget..."
- "This morning at 9:15am in the shared kitchen..."
- "During the client call on November 15th..."

**‚ùå Bad Examples:**
- "Lately..." (too vague)
- "The other day..." (which day?)
- "In meetings..." (which meeting?)
- "You always..." (judgment, not situation)

**Test:** Could you find this moment on a calendar?

---

### B - BEHAVIOR

**What:** Describe what you saw/heard (observable only)

**‚úÖ Good Examples:**
- "When you interrupted me 3 times during my presentation..."
- "When you sent that email at 11pm and marked it urgent..."
- "When you said 'that's a stupid idea' to Sarah..."
- "When you left the wet towel on the bed..."

**‚ùå Bad Examples:**
- "Your attitude was negative..." (not observable)
- "You were being disrespectful..." (interpretation)
- "You didn't care about the deadline..." (assumes intent)
- "You were lazy..." (character judgment)

**Test:** Could a camera capture this? If not, it's not behavior.

**What You Can Observe:**
- Words spoken (exact quotes)
- Actions taken
- Facial expressions
- Body language
- Timing/frequency

**What You Can't Observe:**
- Intentions
- Feelings
- Thoughts
- Character traits

---

### I - IMPACT

**What:** Explain the effect on you/team/results

**‚úÖ Good Examples:**
- "...I felt disrespected and stopped sharing my ideas."
- "...the team missed the deadline and we lost the ‚Ç¨50K contract."
- "...I spent 3 hours redoing work that was already complete."
- "...Sarah stayed quiet for the rest of the meeting."

**‚ùå Bad Examples:**
- "It was bad..." (not specific)
- "It bothered me..." (too vague)
- "You made me angry..." (blames them for your emotion)
- "Everyone was upset..." (vague and assumes)

**Three Types of Impact:**

1. **Personal Impact:** "I felt excluded..."
2. **Team Impact:** "The team fell behind schedule..."
3. **Business Impact:** "We missed the revenue target by ‚Ç¨100K..."

**Test:** Can you quantify or clearly describe the consequence?

---

### R - REQUEST

**What:** State specific, actionable change you want

**‚úÖ Good Examples:**
- "Going forward, I'd like you to check with me before presenting my analysis."
- "In future meetings, would you let me finish my sentences?"
- "Could you put wet towels in the hamper after showering?"
- "I need you to send urgent emails only for true emergencies."

**‚ùå Bad Examples:**
- "Just be better..." (not specific)
- "Show more respect..." (too vague)
- "Don't do that again..." (doesn't say what to do instead)
- "Be more professional..." (undefined)

**Request Quality Checklist:**
- [ ] Is it specific?
- [ ] Is it observable?
- [ ] Is it reasonable?
- [ ] Is it actionable?
- [ ] Is it respectful?

**Test:** Could someone else observe whether they followed through?

---

### COMPLETE SBIR EXAMPLE

**Scenario:** Colleague takes credit for your work

**SBIR Delivery:**

**S:** "In Monday's client meeting at 10am..."

**B:** "...when you presented the pricing analysis and said 'I spent all weekend on this,' that was the analysis I sent you Friday with my name on it."

**I:** "I felt invisible and unvalued. I also lost confidence that my work will be recognized, which makes me less motivated to go above and beyond."

**R:** "Going forward, when you present analysis I've done, I'd like you to say 'Sarah provided this analysis' or include my name on the slides."

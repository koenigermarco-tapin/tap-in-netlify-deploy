üìã The OKR Framework

Objectives and Key Results‚Äîthe alignment system that scales.

What Are OKRs?

Objective: What you want to achieve. Qualitative, inspiring, time-bound.

Key Results: How you'll measure progress. Quantitative, specific, measurable.

Intel invented them. Google scaled with them. They work because they force clarity.

Good OKR Example

O

Objective

"Become the market leader in customer satisfaction"

KR1

Key Result 1

Increase NPS score from 32 to 50

KR2

Key Result 2

Reduce average response time from 4 hours to 1 hour

KR3

Key Result 3

Achieve 95% customer retention rate

ü•ã Why OKRs Work

OKRs force you to define success before you start. Just like drilling a technique before a tournament‚Äîyou know exactly what "winning" looks like.

Without clear Key Results, teams debate endlessly about whether they're succeeding. With them, success is binary.

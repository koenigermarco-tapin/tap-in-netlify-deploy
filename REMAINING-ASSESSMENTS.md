# TAP-IN Remaining Assessments

## 1. Leadership Style Assessment

**Duration:** 5-7 minutes  
**XP Reward:** 150  
**Description:** Discover your natural leadership approach—how you inspire, direct, and develop others.

**Questions:** (Scale 1-5: Strongly Disagree to Strongly Agree)

1. I'm energized by painting a big-picture vision of what's possible
2. I prefer to develop people through questions rather than giving answers
3. I seek input from the team before making major decisions
4. I lead by example and expect others to match my standards
5. In a crisis, I make quick decisions and expect immediate execution
6. I connect daily work to a larger mission and purpose
7. I invest significant time in one-on-one development conversations
8. I build consensus through collaboration and listening to all voices
9. I set ambitious goals and get frustrated when people don't keep up
10. When things go wrong, I take charge and issue clear directives
11. I inspire people by helping them see possibilities they didn't know existed
12. I celebrate when people figure things out on their own rather than being told

**Results Interpretation:**

### **Visionary Leader** (High scores on Q1, Q6, Q11)
You lead through inspiration and possibility. Like **Steve Jobs** or **Elon Musk**, you see the future others can't and make people believe in it. You're the "why" person—connecting work to meaning.

**Strengths:** Inspiring, strategic, innovative, big-picture thinker  
**Watch out for:** Losing patience with details, moving too fast, leaving people behind  
**Famous example:** **Phil Jackson** (NBA) - Created a championship culture through vision, purpose, and triangle offense philosophy  

**Next steps:**  
- Partner with detail-oriented executors to balance vision with implementation
- Slow down to ensure people understand the "how," not just the "why"
- Practice celebrating small wins, not just the big vision

---

### **Coach Leader** (High scores on Q2, Q7, Q12)
You develop people through questions and guidance. Like **John Wooden** or **Bill Campbell** (Silicon Valley's legendary coach), you believe growth happens when people discover answers themselves.

**Strengths:** Develops talent, asks powerful questions, patient, builds capability  
**Watch out for:** Moving too slow when urgency is needed, over-coaching when people need direction  
**Famous example:** **Gregg Popovich** (Spurs) - Legendary for developing players through tough love, questions, and trust  

**Next steps:**  
- Balance coaching with clear direction when the situation demands it
- Set hard deadlines to avoid analysis paralysis
- Know when to shift from "guide" mode to "decide" mode

---

### **Democratic Leader** (High scores on Q3, Q8)
You build buy-in through collaboration and inclusion. Like **Satya Nadella** (Microsoft), you believe the best decisions come from listening to all voices and building consensus.

**Strengths:** Inclusive, collaborative, builds trust, reduces blind spots  
**Watch out for:** Slow decision-making, avoiding tough calls, seeking approval too much  
**Famous example:** **Pete Carroll** (Seahawks) - Built championship teams through player input, collaboration, and shared leadership  

**Next steps:**  
- Set decision deadlines to avoid endless discussion
- Practice making unpopular calls when needed
- Balance collaboration with decisive action

---

### **Pacesetting Leader** (High scores on Q4, Q9)
You lead by example and expect excellence. Like **Kobe Bryant** or **Michael Jordan**, you set the bar through your own performance and expect others to rise to it.

**Strengths:** High standards, leads by example, drives results, relentless  
**Watch out for:** Burning out your team, micromanaging, expecting everyone to work like you  
**Famous example:** **Bill Belichick** (Patriots) - Obsessive preparation, leads by example, expects everyone to "do their job"  

**Next steps:**  
- Communicate *why* the standard matters, not just *what* it is
- Recognize different people have different work styles
- Balance intensity with recovery and celebration

---

### **Commanding Leader** (High scores on Q5, Q10)
You lead through decisiveness and control. Like **Vince Lombardi** or **General Patton**, you make the tough calls, give clear orders, and expect execution.

**Strengths:** Decisive, clear direction, thrives in crisis, drives accountability  
**Watch out for:** Stifling creativity, killing morale, creating dependency  
**Famous example:** **Nick Saban** (Alabama) - Demands discipline, clear systems, and relentless execution. "The Process."  

**Next steps:**  
- Develop others' decision-making skills—don't create dependency
- Balance command with collaboration in stable times
- Explain the "why" behind decisions to build understanding

## 2. Communication Style Assessment

**Duration:** 4 minutes  
**XP Reward:** 100  
**Description:** Understand how you naturally express ideas, process information, and connect with others.

**Questions:** (Scale 1-5: Strongly Disagree to Strongly Agree)

1. I get to the point quickly in conversations—no need for small talk
2. I think out loud and process ideas by talking through them with others
3. I prefer texting or email over phone calls or face-to-face conversations
4. I rely heavily on data, facts, and evidence when making my case
5. I don't mind creating tension if it means addressing the real issue
6. I carefully choose my words to maintain harmony and avoid conflict
7. I pause and think before speaking—I rarely blurt things out
8. I express excitement and emotion openly when I communicate
9. I ask a lot of clarifying questions before forming my opinion
10. I adjust my communication style based on who I'm talking to

**Results Interpretation:**

### **Direct Communicator** (High scores on Q1, Q5, low on Q6)
You're **Kobe Bryant** post-game—honest, blunt, no BS. You value clarity over politeness and expect others to do the same.

**Strengths:** Clear, efficient, no ambiguity, fast decisions  
**Watch out for:** Coming across as harsh, damaging relationships, missing emotional cues  

**Next steps:**  
- Add 10 seconds of context before delivering tough messages
- Say "Here's what I appreciate..." before "Here's what needs to change..."
- Recognize not everyone processes feedback like you do

---

### **Diplomatic Communicator** (High scores on Q6, Q10, low on Q1, Q5)
You're **Barack Obama**—smooth, thoughtful, considerate. You build bridges and keep people comfortable.

**Strengths:** Builds trust, avoids unnecessary conflict, inclusive, adaptable  
**Watch out for:** Avoiding hard conversations, being too indirect, slowing decisions  

**Next steps:**  
- Practice "kind candor"—you can be direct AND compassionate
- Set a personal rule: address issues within 24 hours
- Remember: clarity is kindness, vagueness is cruelty

---

### **Analytical Communicator** (High scores on Q3, Q4, Q7, Q9)
You're **Bill Gates** in a meeting—data-driven, logical, precise. You prepare thoroughly and value accuracy.

**Strengths:** Credible, well-reasoned, minimizes errors, thorough  
**Watch out for:** Over-analyzing, boring people with details, missing the human element  

**Next steps:**  
- Lead with the headline, then dive into data if asked
- Balance logic with storytelling—numbers + narrative = influence
- Practice making gut calls when data is incomplete

---

### **Expressive Communicator** (High scores on Q2, Q8, Q10)
You're **Dwayne "The Rock" Johnson**—animated, energetic, engaging. You light up the room.

**Strengths:** Engaging, builds energy, inspires action, connects emotionally  
**Watch out for:** Dominating conversations, overwhelming introverts, talking more than listening  

**Next steps:**  
- After you speak, count to 5 before speaking again
- Ask "What do you think?" and actually wait for the answer
- Match energy to the room—not everyone needs maximum intensity

## 3. Decision Making Style Assessment

**Duration:** 8-10 minutes  
**XP Reward:** 150  
**Description:** Discover how you make choices under pressure—your decision-making default mode.

**Questions:** (Scale 1-5: Strongly Disagree to Strongly Agree)

1. I trust my gut instincts more than spreadsheets when making big calls
2. I feel paralyzed when I don't have enough data to analyze
3. I prefer to make decisions collaboratively with input from others
4. When the clock is running out, I make the call—no hesitation
5. I often regret decisions I made too quickly without thinking them through
6. My best decisions come from quiet reflection, not rushed conversations
7. I've learned to trust my intuition—it's usually right
8. I need to see the numbers before I feel comfortable deciding
9. I build consensus before moving forward on important choices
10. I'd rather make a fast "good enough" decision than a slow perfect one

**Results Interpretation:**

### **Gut-Instinct Decision Maker** (High scores on Q1, Q7, low on Q2, Q8)
You're **Malcolm Gladwell's "Blink"** in action. Like **Steve Jobs** choosing products or **Michael Jordan** taking the last shot, you trust pattern recognition over analysis.

**Strengths:** Fast decisions, confident, good with incomplete info, decisive under pressure  
**Watch out for:** Overconfidence, missing blind spots, ignoring contrary data  
**Famous example:** **Magic Johnson**—made split-second decisions on the court that looked like telepathy  

**Next steps:**  
- Build a "pre-mortem" habit: before deciding, ask "What could go wrong?"
- Surround yourself with analytical people who challenge your gut
- Track your gut decisions—when are you right vs. wrong?

---

### **Analytical Decision Maker** (High scores on Q2, Q8, low on Q1, Q7)
You're **Moneyball's Billy Beane**—data-driven, methodical, evidence-based. You want to see the numbers before you commit.

**Strengths:** Minimize risk, well-reasoned, credible, thorough  
**Watch out for:** Analysis paralysis, missing the moment, over-thinking simple decisions  
**Famous example:** **Bill Belichick**—obsessive film study, data analysis, preparation over gut feel  

**Next steps:**  
- Set decision deadlines: "I'll decide by Friday, even if data is incomplete"
- Practice 80/20 rule: decide with 80% certainty, not 100%
- Build your intuition muscle with low-stakes decisions

---

### **Collaborative Decision Maker** (High scores on Q3, Q9)
You're **Gregg Popovich** in a timeout—listening, gathering input, building buy-in before the play call.

**Strengths:** Inclusive, reduces blind spots, builds commitment, diverse perspectives  
**Watch out for:** Slow decisions, seeking approval, avoiding accountability  
**Famous example:** **Phil Jackson**—famous for letting players self-organize and collaborate on solutions  

**Next steps:**  
- Set clear decision owners: "I'm gathering input, but I'm making the call"
- Use collaboration for *important* decisions, not *urgent* ones
- Practice making solo calls on low-stakes decisions

---

### **Decisive Action-Taker** (High scores on Q4, Q10)
You're **Tom Brady** in the 2-minute drill—time's running out, you're making the call, let's go.

**Strengths:** Fast execution, momentum, confidence, clarity  
**Watch out for:** Rushed mistakes, skipping important input, creating chaos  
**Famous example:** **Kobe Bryant**—"I'd rather go 0-for-30 than 0-for-9. 0-for-9 means you quit."  

**Next steps:**  
- Build a 5-minute pause for big decisions: "Let me think on this and circle back"
- Ask "What am I missing?" before pulling the trigger
- Balance speed with smart process on high-stakes calls

## 4. Values Discovery Assessment

**Duration:** 10-15 minutes  
**XP Reward:** 200  
**Description:** Identify your core values—what truly matters to you and drives your decisions.

**Part 1: Rating Questions** (Scale 1-5: Not Important to Extremely Important)

1. Achieving ambitious goals and hitting new milestones matters deeply to me
2. I'm energized by learning, growing, and becoming better than I was yesterday
3. Having control over my time and decisions is essential to my happiness
4. Deep, meaningful relationships are more important than achievements
5. Making a lasting difference in the world drives my daily work
6. I'm motivated by doing things at the highest possible standard
7. Having work-life harmony and avoiding burnout is a top priority
8. Trying new things and stepping outside my comfort zone excites me
9. Financial stability and safety give me peace of mind
10. Expressing myself creatively and thinking outside the box fulfills me
11. Being recognized and respected for my accomplishments matters to me
12. Helping others reach their potential brings me deep satisfaction

**Part 2: Forced Ranking Exercise**

Rank these 10 values from most important (1) to least important (10):

- **Achievement:** Hitting goals, winning, accomplishing hard things
- **Growth:** Learning, improving, becoming better
- **Freedom:** Autonomy, flexibility, control over your life
- **Connection:** Relationships, belonging, teamwork
- **Impact:** Making a difference, leaving a legacy
- **Excellence:** Mastery, quality, doing things right
- **Balance:** Harmony, rest, sustainability
- **Adventure:** Novelty, risk, exploration
- **Security:** Stability, safety, predictability
- **Creativity:** Innovation, expression, originality

**Results Interpretation:**

### **Achievement-Driven** (High Q1, Q11; Top-ranked: Achievement, Excellence)
You're wired like **Kobe Bryant** or **Serena Williams**—winning matters. You measure life by milestones hit and standards exceeded.

**Your motto:** "Second place is first loser."  
**Danger zone:** Burnout, never feeling satisfied, relationships suffer  
**Next steps:**  
- Define what "enough" looks like so you can celebrate wins
- Build relationships that aren't transactional
- Practice gratitude for what you've already achieved

---

### **Growth-Focused** (High Q2, Q12; Top-ranked: Growth, Impact)
You're **Carol Dweck's growth mindset** personified. Like **LeBron James** constantly evolving his game, you're never done learning.

**Your motto:** "I'm not who I was last year—and I won't be the same next year."  
**Danger zone:** Constant self-criticism, never feeling "arrived"  
**Next steps:**  
- Celebrate milestones, not just the climb
- Track your growth trajectory to see progress
- Help others grow—teaching reinforces your learning

---

### **Freedom-Seeker** (High Q3; Top-ranked: Freedom, Adventure)
You're **Anthony Bourdain** or **Alex Honnold**—autonomy over everything. You'll sacrifice money for control over your time.

**Your motto:** "Don't tell me what to do."  
**Danger zone:** Avoiding commitment, struggles with structure, lone wolf syndrome  
**Next steps:**  
- Find teams/companies that value autonomy
- Build systems that create freedom, not chaos
- Recognize some structure actually enables more freedom

---

### **Connection-Oriented** (High Q4, Q12; Top-ranked: Connection, Balance)
You're **Brené Brown** or **Ted Lasso**—relationships are everything. Work is just the vehicle for connection.

**Your motto:** "People over projects."  
**Danger zone:** Avoiding conflict, sacrificing results for harmony, over-giving  
**Next steps:**  
- Practice caring candor—honesty IS love
- Set boundaries so you can sustain relationships
- Remember: great relationships include accountability

---

### **Impact-Driven** (High Q5; Top-ranked: Impact, Growth)
You're **Bill Gates** (post-Microsoft) or **Muhammad Ali**—legacy matters more than money. You want to make a dent.

**Your motto:** "Did I make a difference?"  
**Danger zone:** Impatience with "small" work, burnout, martyr complex  
**Next steps:**  
- Define impact clearly—what does "enough" look like?
- Celebrate small wins that compound into big impact
- Sustain yourself so you can play the long game

---

### **Excellence-Obsessed** (High Q6; Top-ranked: Excellence, Achievement)
You're **Steve Jobs** or **Simone Biles**—quality over everything. You can't tolerate mediocrity.

**Your motto:** "If it's worth doing, it's worth doing right."  
**Danger zone:** Perfectionism, diminishing returns, driving people away

- Celebrate "good enough" on low-stakes work
- Recognize people, not just output

You're the **anti-hustle culture**. Like **Tim Ferriss** (4-Hour Workweek), you optimize for sustainability, not burnout.

**Your motto:** "Work to live, don't live to work."  
**Danger zone:** Avoiding challenges, comfort zone addiction, under-achieving potential  
**Next steps:**  
- Define what balance *actually* means for you (it's personal)

- Challenge yourself periodically to stay sharp

You're **Richard Branson** or **Bear Grylls**—novelty, risk, exploration. Routine is death.

**Your motto:** "Life's too short for boring."  
**Danger zone:** Commitment issues, chasing shiny objects, lack of depth

---

**Your motto:** "Slow and steady wins the race."  
**Danger zone:** Risk-avoidance, missing opportunities, playing too safe  
**Next steps:**  
- Take calculated risks in low-stakes areas to build confidence
- Define "enough" security—when does more stop mattering?
- Remember: biggest risk is sometimes taking NO risk

---

### **Creative Innovator** (High Q10; Top-ranked: Creativity, Adventure)
You're **Lin-Manuel Miranda** or **Steve Jobs**—originality matters. You need to create, not replicate.

**Your motto:** "Why do it like everyone else?"  
**Danger zone:** Reinventing wheels, undervaluing proven approaches, chaos  
**Next steps:**

- Collaborate with executors who can implement your ideas
- Recognize when "good enough" beats "revolutionary"

Deep Work & Focus Architecture

Why This Matters

Deep work is the ability to focus without distraction on cognitively demanding tasks. In a world of constant interruptions, this is a superpower. "Clarity about what matters provides clarity about what does not." – Cal Newport

Key Concepts

  • Time Blocking: Scheduling focused work sessions in your calendar.
  • Context Switching Costs: Every interruption can cost 23 minutes of lost focus.
  • Distraction-Free Environments: Designing your space and tech for focus.
  • Flow States: The psychology of optimal experience and productivity.
  • Attention Residue: Unfinished tasks linger in your mind, reducing focus.

Practical Tools

  • Focus Session Planner: Plan and review your deep work blocks. Download PDF
  • Distraction Audit: Identify and eliminate your top 3 distractions.
  • Flow Rituals: Pre-work routines to trigger focus (music, environment, cues).
  • Attention Reset Techniques: Mindfulness, breathing, or movement to clear mental residue.

Action Plan

  1. Schedule at least one 60-90 minute deep work block per day.
  2. Complete a distraction audit and remove or reduce your top 3 distractions.
  3. Design a flow ritual to start each deep work session.
  4. Use the Focus Session Planner to track your progress.
  5. Practice an attention reset technique after each session.
  6. Review your output and focus quality at the end of each day.
  7. Adjust your environment and rituals based on what works best.

Progress Tracking

Track the number of deep work hours and your focus quality (1-10) daily. Celebrate each session completed without interruption!

Energy Management Fundamentals

Why This Matters

Managing your energy—not just your time—is the foundation of sustainable high performance. Research shows that energy, not hours, is the limiting factor for productivity, creativity, and well-being. "The ultimate measure of our lives is not how much time we spend, but how much energy we invest in the time we have." – Tony Schwartz

Key Concepts

  • Ultradian Rhythms: Your body cycles through 90-120 minute energy peaks and troughs. Work with, not against, these cycles.
  • Energy Audit: Identify what activities energize or drain you. Awareness is the first step to change.
  • Peak Performance Windows: Schedule your most important work during your natural energy highs.
  • Active Recovery: Short, intentional breaks (walks, breathing, movement) restore energy faster than passive rest.
  • Energy Multipliers: Sleep, nutrition, movement, and meaningful connection are the four pillars of energy renewal.

Practical Tools

  • Personal Energy Audit Worksheet: Track your energy levels and activities for 3 days. Download PDF
  • 90-Minute Focus Timer: Use a timer to work in 90-minute blocks, then take a 10-15 minute break.
  • Recovery Menu: Create a list of 5 quick activities that reliably recharge you (e.g., walk, music, stretching).
  • Energy Rituals: Morning and evening routines to prime and restore your energy.

Action Plan

  1. Complete the Personal Energy Audit for 3 days.
  2. Identify your peak energy windows and schedule your most important work during these times.
  3. Set a 90-minute timer for focused work, followed by a 10-15 minute active recovery break.
  4. Build a "Recovery Menu" and use it at least twice daily.
  5. Establish a simple morning and evening energy ritual (e.g., 5-minute stretch, gratitude journal).
  6. Track your energy and mood at the end of each day.
  7. Review your progress after 7 days and adjust your routines as needed.

Progress Tracking

Rate your energy (1-10) and mood at the end of each day. Note what worked and what didn’t. After 7 days, compare your baseline to your current state. Celebrate small wins!

Boundary Setting & Communication

Why This Matters

Healthy boundaries are the foundation of sustainable performance and well-being. Clear communication protects your time, energy, and relationships. "Daring to set boundaries is about having the courage to love ourselves, even when we risk disappointing others." – Brené Brown

Key Concepts

  • Strategic "No": Saying no to protect your priorities and mental capacity.
  • Boundary Scripts: Pre-prepared phrases for common situations (e.g., "I can't take this on right now").
  • Stakeholder Management: Aligning expectations with colleagues, managers, and family.
  • Guilt-Free Boundaries: Reframing boundaries as acts of self-respect, not selfishness.
  • Early Warning Systems: Recognizing signs of boundary erosion before burnout hits.

Practical Tools

  • Boundary Audit Worksheet: Identify where your boundaries are strong or weak. Download PDF
  • Boundary Scripts Library: Ready-to-use phrases for saying no, negotiating, and resetting expectations.
  • Stakeholder Map: Visualize your key relationships and where boundaries are needed.
  • Boundary Rituals: Daily or weekly check-ins to reinforce your limits.

Action Plan

  1. Complete the Boundary Audit to identify your biggest challenges.
  2. Choose 2-3 boundary scripts and practice them in real conversations.
  3. Map your key stakeholders and clarify expectations with at least one person.
  4. Set a recurring calendar reminder for a weekly boundary check-in.
  5. Notice and document any guilt or discomfort—reframe as self-respect.
  6. Track your energy and stress levels before and after setting boundaries.
  7. Review your progress after 7 days and adjust scripts or rituals as needed.

Progress Tracking

Note your confidence and comfort with boundaries each day (1-10). Track any changes in energy, stress, or relationships. Celebrate each successful boundary!

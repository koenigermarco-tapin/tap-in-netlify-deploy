Feedback & Communication

Why This Matters

Feedback and communication are the engines of growth, trust, and collaboration. High-performing teams give and receive feedback frequently and skillfully. "The single biggest problem in communication is the illusion that it has taken place." – George Bernard Shaw

Key Concepts

  • Radical Candor: Caring personally while challenging directly (Kim Scott).
  • Feedforward: Focusing on future improvement, not just past mistakes.
  • Active Listening: Listening to understand, not just to reply.
  • Psychological Safety: Creating an environment where people feel safe to speak up.
  • Feedback Rituals: Making feedback a regular, normalized part of work.

Practical Tools

  • Feedback Script Library: Ready-to-use phrases for giving and receiving feedback.
  • Feedforward Worksheet: Guide for future-focused feedback. Download PDF
  • Active Listening Checklist: Steps to ensure you’re truly hearing others.
  • Feedback Calendar: Schedule regular feedback moments (weekly, project-end, etc.).

Action Plan

  1. Practice giving feedback using the Radical Candor model.
  2. Ask for feedforward from a colleague or friend.
  3. Use the Active Listening Checklist in your next conversation.
  4. Schedule a feedback ritual (e.g., Friday feedback, project debrief).
  5. Reflect on how feedback was received and what you learned.
  6. Track your comfort and skill with feedback over the week.
  7. Adjust your approach based on what works best for you and your team.

Progress Tracking

Rate your comfort with giving and receiving feedback (1-10) daily. Note any improvements in relationships or team performance. Celebrate each feedback win!

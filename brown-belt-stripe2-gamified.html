Every organization has a culture. Few design it intentionally.

The Culture Equation

Culture = What you tolerate + What you celebrate + What you repeat.

It's not your mission statement. It's not your values poster. It's what actually happens when nobody's watching.

‚¨õ Black Belt Wisdom

"Culture is not what you say. It's what you tolerate."

The behavior you ignore is the behavior you endorse. Every time you let something slide, you're teaching the organization what's acceptable.

ü•ã The BJJ Principle: Gym Culture

Walk into any BJJ gym and you'll feel the culture instantly. Is it competitive or collaborative? Ego-driven or ego-free? Do higher belts help lower belts, or crush them?

That culture wasn't an accident. The head coach designed it‚Äîthrough who they promoted, what they celebrated, and what they refused to tolerate.

üìä The Research

Edgar Schein, the father of organizational culture, identified three levels: Artifacts (what you see), Espoused Values (what you say), and Basic Assumptions (what you actually believe).

Most culture change fails because it targets artifacts and values but ignores the underlying assumptions that drive real behavior.
